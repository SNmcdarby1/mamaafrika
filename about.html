African fabric

Each part of Africa has a specific type of Fabric that is worn in each country. In the West African, and Central Africa they have a specific fabric like sarong, often worn by women and wrapped around the chest or waist, over the head as a headscarf, or as a baby sling. Kitenges are like kangas and kikoy but are of a thicker cloth and have an edging along each side. Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Sudan, Nigeria, Cameroon, Ghana, Senegal, Liberia, Rwanda, and Democratic Republic of the Congo are some of the African countries where kitenge is worn.

Art Work

The printing on the cloth is done by a traditional batik technique. These are known as wax prints and the design is equally as bright and detailed on the obverse side of the fabric that can also be framed or otherwise hung up on the wall as a decorative batik artwork. Many of the designs have a meaning. A large variety of religiou political designs are found as well as traditional tribal patterns. The cloth is used as material for dresses, blouses, and pants as well.
